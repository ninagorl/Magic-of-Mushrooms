Psilocybin
Mushrooms

Psilocybin is a hallucinogen that people can ingest through
certain types of mushrooms. Psilocybin works by activating
serotonin receptors, most often in the prefrontal cortex.
This part of the brain affects mood, cognition, and percep-
tion. Hallucinogens also work in other regions of the brain
that regulate arousal and panic responses.Psilocybin is much
more of a promising treatment for a range of health issues.
In particular, experts increasingly see the chemical as a poten-
tially effective, low-risk tool to help patients break their depen-
dencies on other substances and disorders. Given that more
than 100,000 people died after overdosing on opioids and other
drugs in the U.S. last year, it’s an understatement to say it’s
urgent to find new, effective treatments for various disorders.

Psilocybe
Cubensis

Psilocybe Cubensis is probably the image that comes to
mind when we think of psychedelic mushrooms. "Cubes"
are amongst the most widely distributed species because
of their easy-to-cultivate properties, naturally germinating
in cattle dung and grown indoors with substances like
coffee or brown rice flour. It is known as a versatile species.

Psilocybe Mexicana

Psilocybe Mexicana still grows in Mexico during the rainy
season, especially in Oaxaca, Michoacán, Puebla, and others.
The species prefers to live in moss, meadows, deciduous
forests, and soils rich in manure, as well as alongside roads
and trails—but never directly on dung. In Mexico today,
they’re often called “Pajaritos” meaning “little birds” for pack-
ing such a potent experience into a small, fragile mushroom.

Psilocybe
Caerulescens

Derrumbes are still used ceremoniously by the Mazatec
people of Oaxaca, Mexico, and continue to grow in the
Sierra Madre mountain range. Derrumbes are small, and
have a silvery-blue metallic luster that makes them easy
to differentiate from other species. Their potency is low to
moderate, and the trip can even be a bit shorter, lasting
from three to six hours.

B+ Cubensis

B+ features a large, lengthy, yellow-white fruiting body, and
its hallucinogenic effects are known to usher perceptual feel-
ings of warmth and ease. B+ has become the ideal beginner
strain since its effects are more moderate and toned-down,
as the name implies. Another compliment to the B+ strain is
its supernatural ability to reduce side effects of nausea,
associated with most other strains of mushrooms.

Liberty Caps

Liberty caps are the smallest of the top few most potent
psilocybin mushrooms. They have a conical or bell-shaped
cap, hence their name, and they reportedly taste similar to
flour. Most Liberty Caps that are consumed are picked in
the wild. But be careful when identifying because they can
easily be confused with a few similar-looking poisonous
species that grow in the same areas.

Flying Saucers

Flying saucers—named for their unique UFO-like shape—
are known for their intense visuals and profound inner
journeys. The surface of the cap is smooth, with a viscous
appearance when moist, feeling sticky to the touch, with a
separable gelatinous pellicle that can be removed if
the cap is broken carefully.

Pink Buffalo

Sometimes known as “Thai Pink Buffalo”, this strain was
named by ethnomycologist John Allen on the lush Thai
island of Koh Samui because it was first spotted next to
a Pink Buffalo. It remains a popular strain for home cul-
tivators because it colonizes quickly and fruits abundantly
if conditions are right.

Golden Teachers

Golden Teacher is amongst the most favored strains of psyc-
hedelic mushrooms. Golden Teachers make their claim to
fame by depositing a palpable sense of ancient intelligence
through consoling feelings of heart-centered warmth and love.
A sense of educational clarity is often reported with their exp-
eriences, offering the perfect balance of consciousness expan-
sion and euphoric enjoyment.

Wavy Caps

Though these mighty mushrooms are under the category of
fungi that thrive on the surface of decaying wood, Wavy Caps
deserve a spotlight of their own. As its name suggests, these
mushrooms boast a large, flared, umbrella-like cap with ripp-
led ridges. If you’re concerned with the potential of experien-
cing wood-lover’s paralysis, you’d be happy to hear that Wavy
Caps eliminate the potential for experiencing any somatic
motor complications.

Blue Meanies

Blue Meanies’ dramatic potency of this strain produces power-
ful visual and auditory stimulations that melody throughout
your journey. These bare-striped mushrooms nourish their
human counterparts with an energetic vigor of wide-reaching
awareness and are no stranger to the psychedelic antics of our
modern society. These blue-staining fungi offer an average of
2.5% psilocybin, making them upwards of 3 times more potent
than psilocybe cubensis.
